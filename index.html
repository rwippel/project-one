Tom Petty

October 20, 1950 - October 2, 2017

Thomas Earl Petty was an American singer, songwriter and music producer from Gainesville, Florida. Best known as the lead singer of his band, Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers. In 1974, he moved to Los Angeles to pursue his passion for music, where he became very successful. His music has sold over 20 million copies in the United States alone. His career started with the first band he was a part, Mudcrutch, which evolved in to Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers. Petty was also co-founder of the group the Traveling Wilbury's with Bob Dylan. He died weeks after his 40th Anniversary tour at the age of 66, leaving fans devastated.
He lives on through his music.

Achievements

  • Tom Petty & the Heartbreakers received a Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 1999.
  • Inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame in New York in 2002.
  • He received the Billboard Century Award in 2005.
