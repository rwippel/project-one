Tom Petty

October 20, 1950 - October 2, 2017 (66)

Hometown Gainesville, Florida

Thomas Earl Petty was an American singer, songwriter and music producer from Florida, best known from being the lead singer of his band, Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers. In 1974, he moved to Los Angeles to pursue his passion for music, where he became very successful. His music has sold over 20 million copies in the United States alone. One of the first bands he was a part of was called Mudcrutch, which evolved in to Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers. Petty was also co-founder of the group the Traveling Wilbury's with Bob Dylan. Petty died weeks after his 40th Anniversary tour at the age of 66, leaving fans devastated. He lives on through his music.

Achievements

  • Inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame in New York in 2002.
  • Tom Petty & the Heartbreakers received a Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 1999.
  • Tom Petty received the Billboard Century Award in 2005.
